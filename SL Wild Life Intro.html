DIFFERENT LOCATIONS FOR WILDLIFE IN SRI LANKA

Sinharaja Forest

Sinharaja Forest Reserve, located in the southwestern part of Sri Lanka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the last remaining primary rainforests in the country. Spread across the districts of Ratnapura, Galle, and Matara, Sinharaja is a biodiversity hotspot, renowned for its unique and rich ecosystem. The name "Sinharaja" translates to "Lion Kingdom" in Sinhalese, emphasizing its importance and regal status

Udawalawa National Park

Udawalawe National Park, situated in the southern part of Sri Lanka, is a renowned wildlife sanctuary known for its diverse range of flora and fauna. The park was established in 1972 with the primary purpose of providing a sanctuary for wild animals displaced by the construction of the Udawalawe Reservoir. Covering an area of approximately 308 square kilometers, Udawalawe is one of the best places in Sri Lanka to observe elephants in their natural habitat

Horton Plains

Horton Plains National Park, situated in the central highlands of Sri Lanka, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its unique landscapes, biodiversity, and breathtaking views. Established in 1988, the park covers an expanse of approximately 31,000 acres and is characterized by its rolling hills, grassy plains, and cloud forests. One of the key attractions within the park is the precipice known as "World's End," offering stunning panoramic views of the surrounding areas.

Minneriya National Park

Minneriya National Park, located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, is a popular wildlife destination known for its diverse ecosystems and the famous Elephant Gathering that occurs annually.
