Indigenous Animals in SriLanka

Sri Lanka is home to a rich array of indigenous animals that have adapted to the diverse ecosystems found on the island. These indigenous species include a variety of mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish. Some examples include:

  • Toque Macaque (Macaca sinica)
  • Sri Lankan Sloth Bear (Melursus ursinus inornatus)
  • Purple-faced Langur (Semnopithecus vetulus)
  • Ceylonese Wood Pigeon (Columba torringtoni)
  • Hump-nosed Lizard (Lyriocephalus scutatus)

These indigenous animals contribute to the unique biodiversity of Sri Lanka, and conservation efforts are essential to safeguard their habitats and ensure their long-term survival. Protected areas, national parks, and wildlife reserves play a crucial role in preserving the indigenous fauna of the island.
